Volunteering for RPG Limit Break

Volunteer Roles for RPG Limit Break are categorized into 2 Departments; The Technical Department and The Community Department.

The Technical Department Roles cover the technical side of the Marathon and are: Setup and Tear Down, Stream Tech, Stage Tech, Audio Tech and Interview Tech.

The Community Department Roles cover the forward facing side of the Marathon and are: Hosting, Donation Station, Interview, Safety, Check-In, Cleaning, Moderation, and Social Media.

For more information on these roles; Read on!

Tech Department Volunteer Roles

Setup & Tear Down – This role is to help the Tech Team setup / tear down the event spaces on the Friday and Saturday before the event begins, and the Sunday after the event ends. This role will check in with Logistics Staff to maintain our inventory spreadsheet throughout the process.

Stream Tech – (Scheduled by game) Stream Technicians maintain the Stream PC and Recording PC for the stream. They also facilitate the completion of game transitions by communicating with the on shift Producer to ensure all roles have completed their checklist items. Your duties will include, but are not limited to, monitoring the Stream PC to verify the quality of the stream is good and making sure correct layouts are utilized from run to run. As a jack of all trades the Stream Tech is a great introduction to volunteering if technology is your interest. OBS familiarity is a plus, but is not required.

Stream Tech is a great first tech role to volunteer for our event.

Stage Tech – (Scheduled by game) Stage Technicians prepare the next run on stage, by connecting consoles, verifying equipment is working, moving chairs, cleaning and handing out headsets to runners, commentators, and volunteers and adjusting the stage camera to provide the best view for our online audience. They work with the Producer and the Stream Tech to ensure everything is working and that the run can proceed. Familiarity with Steam and with connecting consoles is a big plus, but is not required.

Audio Tech – (Scheduled by game) Audio Technicians are responsible for all the sound, whether it goes to the stream, to the live audience, and even to the headsets of the runners and commentators. They will work under the direction of the Producer to set the relative levels of all audio sources, transition the audio feed between scenes, and maintain a clear and comfortable listening experience for all viewers. Familiarity with digital audio mixers is required. Specific knowledge of the Behringer Wing is beneficial, but not necessary.

This role has typically received lower application numbers than others, please consider applying!

Interview Tech – (Scheduled per Interview) Interview Tech volunteers will be required to help setup interviews. This includes, but is not limited to, adjusting audio so the interview participants have the correct audio levels, adjusting the Interview camera to make sure the interview is in frame, and coordinating with the prize team to ensure all relevant prizes are displayed during the interview. You will also be present during the interview to give a point of sight to help keep interviews focused on you and not the camera. You will need to be sure the interviewee and interviewer are on site and ready for the interview coming up.

Interview Tech is a great tech role to volunteer for if you want to work your way up to being an Audio Tech Volunteer.

Community Department Volunteer Roles

Hosting – (Scheduled by game) Our hosts for the event are responsible for being the on-air voice during the event that conveys a multitude of information to the runners and the viewing audience. This information includes (but is not limited to): donation comments, “blurbs” about the event and/or charity, upcoming runs, incentive/bidwar updates, and available prizes. Hosts are also responsible for introducing both the run(s) for which they will host, as well as any interviews occurring during their shift. Hosting shifts are connected to a particular game or block of games, and typically last for about 2-4 hours; if you are assigned to a longer game it will be split among multiple hosts.

You do not need to be an experienced host to apply.
For RPG Limit Break 2023 we will be accepting Sign Ups for Remote Hosts. See note at top of page

Donation Station - (Scheduled by game) Donation Station volunteers are responsible for processing the donations we receive on behalf of NAMI. This includes, but is not limited to, screening donation comments and commenter names to chose which to send to the hosts, approving new suggestions for naming incentives, and helping donators apply their donations to appropriate incentives.

Donation Station is a great first Community department role to volunteer for our event.
For RPG Limit Break 2023 we will be accepting Sign Ups for Remote Hosts. See note at top of page

Interview - Interview volunteers help with planning and conducting the interviews you see on screen during the marathon with Runners, staff and Representatives of our charities.
For RPG Limit Break 2023 we will not be accepting new Interview volunteer sign ups

You will be added to Tweetdeck in order to tweet from our account and receive all the information and training you will need to be successful!
This is a Remote Volunteer Role

Safety – (Scheduled in 4 hour shifts) The role of the safety team is to ensure a safe environment for all attendees at the event. Our focus is to know and understand the rules and report any issues or concerns to the Safety Coordinator and Staff along with gentle reminders to people to abide by the rules of the event when we see violations.

Check-In – (Scheduled in 4 hour shifts) In addition to checking attendees into the event, Check-In volunteers are responsible for managing the lost and found and otherwise assisting attendees trying to find their way about the event. We’re looking for people who are comfortable with interacting with and helping others.

This role has typically received lower application numbers than others, please consider applying!
Check-In is a great first Community department role to volunteer for our event.

Cleaning - (Scheduled in 4 hour shifts) The Cleaning Crew is responsible for keeping our event space safe and welcoming for all attendees. You’ll be asked to perform rounds of the event space, cleaning surfaces as they are used. While you’ll be on your feet during that part of the shift, you are not expected to clean continuously; there will be downtime.

This role has typically received lower application numbers than others, please consider applying!

Moderation – This role monitors both Twitch chat and our Discord Server during the marathon. Moderating according to our policies and working with other team members to make moderation decisions. You are not expected to be around the stream 24/7.

Please Note: This is NOT a permanent moderation assignment and you may lose your privileges after the marathon concludes!
This is a Remote Volunteer Role

Social Media – Social Media Volunteers, while on duty, will be responsible for managing our Twitter feed. This includes Sending Tweets and Retweeting/Liking posts that are relevant. The Tweets you may be asked to create will include, but are not limited to, Promoting Runs, Updating Donation milestones and engaging with our community all of which will be outlined in more detail in our Volunteer Documentation.

The things you will be on the look out for include, but are not limited to, COVID-19 policies (including mask alignment), ID badges worn in event rooms, and promoting a safe and respectful environment that can be enjoyed by all.

This role has typically received lower application numbers than others, please consider applying!

Hosting Audition Tools

Audio Recording Options

Windows: Use the built in “Voice Recorder” software. You can find it by going to the Start Menu and typing “Voice Recorder”. Press the record button in the middle to start, press again to stop.

Mac: Use QuickTime Player’s built in audio recording option. Go to “File” and then select “New Audio Recording”.

Alternative #1: https://vocaroo.com/ - Simple web based recording website.

Alternative #2: Audacity - A free program for both Windows and Mac. Refer to their documentation for making a voice recording.
