Volunteering for RPG Limit Break

Onsite volunteer positions each have orientations covering how these roles operate during the event. While experience related to each position is a big plus, these orientations are also there to help answer questions and guide first-time volunteers on how to perform the tasks necessary to fulfill their roles.

Volunteer roles for RPG Limit Break are categorized into 2 Departments: the Technical Department and the Community Department.

The Technical Department roles cover the technical side of the marathon and are: Setup and Tear Down, Stream Tech, Stage Tech, and Audio Tech.

The Community Department roles cover the forward facing side of the marathon and are: Hosting, Donation Station, Safety, Check-In, Cleaning, Moderation, and Social Media.

For more information on these roles, read on!

Unless otherwise noted, all departments will be scheduled in 4 hour blocks.

Tech Department Volunteer Roles

Setup & Tear Down

This role is to help the Tech Team setup / tear down the event spaces on the Friday and Saturday before the event begins, and the Sunday after the event ends. This role will check in with Tech Staff to maintain our inventory spreadsheet throughout the process.

Stream Tech

Stream Technicians maintain the Stream PC and Recording PC for the stream. They also facilitate the completion of game transitions by communicating with the on-shift Producer to ensure all roles have completed their checklist items. Your duties will include, but are not limited to, monitoring the Stream PC to verify the quality of the stream is good and making sure correct layouts are utilized from run to run. As a jack-of-all-trades position, the Stream Tech is a great introduction to volunteering if technology is your interest. OBS familiarity is a plus, but is not required.

Stream Tech is a great first tech role to volunteer for our event.

Stage Tech

Scheduled by game

Stage Technicians prepare the next run on stage by connecting consoles, verifying equipment is working, moving chairs, cleaning and handing out headsets to runners, commentators, and volunteers and adjusting the stage camera to provide the best view for our online audience. They work with the Producer and the Stream Tech to ensure everything is working and that the run can proceed. Familiarity with Steam and with connecting consoles is a big plus, but is not required.

Audio Tech

Audio Technicians are responsible for all the sound, whether it goes to the stream, to the live audience, and even to the headsets of the runners and commentators. They will work under the direction of the Producer to set the relative levels of all audio sources, transition the audio feed between scenes, and maintain a clear and comfortable listening experience for all viewers. Familiarity with digital audio mixers is required. Specific knowledge of the Behringer Wing is beneficial, but not necessary.

This role has typically received lower application numbers than others, please consider applying!

Community Department Volunteer Roles

Hosting

Scheduled by game

Volunteers that sign up for the Onsite Host role will primarily be responsible for reading donations and their comments on stream. Additionally, they will read sponsor copy and charity/event information. They will also introduce runs, runners and their commentators, and in some cases will interact with them during the run. Once the run has concluded, hosts will also provide intros/outros for interviews and/or advertisements.

You do not need to be an experienced host to apply!

For {{ speedrunEvent?.name }} we will be accepting sign ups for Remote Hosts, but in an effort to move RPG Limit Break back to a majority onsite event, priority will be given to those who are attending the marathon in person.

Donation Station

Donation Station volunteers are responsible for processing the donations we receive on behalf of NAMI. This includes, but is not limited to: screening donation comments and commenter names to choose which to send to the hosts; approving new name suggestions for naming incentives; and helping apply donors' donations to appropriate incentives.

Donation Station is a great first Community department role to volunteer for our event.

For {{ speedrunEvent?.name }} we will be continuing to prioritize accepting more Remote Donation Station Volunteers, so bear that in mind when applying!

Safety

The role of the safety team is to ensure a safe environment for all attendees at the event. Our focus involves knowing and understanding the rules and reporting any issues or concerns to the Safety Coordinator and Staff along with gentle reminders to attendees to abide by the rules of the event when we see violations.

The things you will be on the look out for include, but are not limited to: ID badges worn in event rooms, matters regarding event health policies, and promoting a safe and respectful environment that can be enjoyed by all.

This role has typically received lower application numbers than others, please consider applying!

Check-In

In addition to checking attendees into the event and giving them their badge, Check-In volunteers are responsible for managing the lost-and-found and otherwise assisting attendees trying to find their way about the event. We're looking for people who are comfortable interacting with and helping others.

This role has typically received lower application numbers than others, please consider applying!

Check-In is a great first Community department role to volunteer for our event.

Cleaning

The Cleaning Crew is responsible for keeping our event spaces clean for all attendees. You'll be asked to perform rounds of the event space, cleaning surfaces as they are used. While you'll be on your feet during that part of the shift, you are not expected to clean continuously; typical shifts consist of a few walkthroughs of event spaces to wipe down and clean surfaces per shift. This includes sections of our tech pit and our host desk, but not our stream main stage or interview set.

This role has typically received lower application numbers than others, please consider applying!

Chat Moderation

This role monitors both Twitch chat and YouTube chat during the marathon, moderating according to our policies and working with other team members to make moderation decisions. You are not expected to be around the stream 24/7.

This is NOT a permanent moderation assignment and you may lose your privileges after the marathon concludes!

This is a Remote Volunteer role.

Social Media

Social Media Volunteers, while on duty, will be responsible for managing our Twitter and Bluesky feeds. This includes: Sending posts and Retweeting/Liking posts that are relevant. The Tweets you may be asked to create will include but are not limited to: promoting runs; updating donation milestones; and engaging with our community, all of which will be outlined in more detail in our Volunteer Documentation.

You will receive all the information, tools and training you will need to be successful!

This is a Remote Volunteer role.

Hosting Audition Tools for Recording Audio

Windows

Use the built in “Voice Recorder” software. You can find it by going to the Start Menu and typing “Voice Recorder”. Press the record button in the middle to start, press again to stop.

Mac

Use QuickTime Player's built in audio recording option. Go to “File” and then select “New Audio Recording”.

Alternatives

Vocaroo – Simple web based recording website.

Audacity – A free program for both Windows and Mac. Refer to their documentation for making a voice recording.
